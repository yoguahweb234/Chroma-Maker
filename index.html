Chróma-Maker

What is this?

Chróma-Maker is a color palette generator that lives entirely in your browser. No sign-ups, no servers, no tracking. You pick a style, hit generate, and get a fresh 5-color palette every time. It's built for designers, developers, or anyone who just likes playing with color.

Palette Modes

Vibrant Pastel Monochromatic Chromahex Random

Each mode uses HSL color math to produce palettes with a distinct feel. Vibrant gives you bold, saturated colors. Pastel keeps things soft and muted. Monochromatic explores a single hue across different lightness levels. Chromahex stays in the cool blue-green range (160-220). Random just throws the dice and sees what happens.

How to Use It

Press Space to generate a new palette, or click the green Generate button. Click any color tile to copy its hex code to your clipboard. Hit Copy All Hexes to grab the entire palette at once. When Monochromatic is selected, you can toggle between D-L (dark to light) and L-D (light to dark) ordering.

Locking Colors

Hover over any color tile and a yellow "Lock this Color" button appears above it. Click it to lock that color in place. Locked colors stay put when you generate new palettes, letting you build up a custom set one color at a time. Switching to Monochromatic mode clears all locks automatically.

Export

Found a palette you like? Export it as SVG for clean vector work, PNG for mockups and presentations, or JPG if that's what your pipeline needs. Everything renders at 2x resolution so it looks crisp on any screen.

Built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No frameworks, no APIs, no dependencies. Just open the file and go.

Chróma-Maker is a simple, no-nonsense color palette generator built right into your browser. Pick a mode that fits your mood - whether you need punchy, eye-catching tones or soft, muted shades - and hit generate. The whole thing was designed with that warm, tactile feel of early smartphone interfaces in mind. Thick glossy buttons, soft inner shadows, and that familiar glass-like shine on every tile. It's a small thing, but we figured it should be pleasant to look at while you're doing something as creative as picking colors. So go ahead, press spacebar a few times, and see what turns up.
